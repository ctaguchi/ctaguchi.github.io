ANLP2021

Oral presentation in NLP for the first time!

  • February 15, 2021

I'll be making a conference presentation at the 27th Annual Meeting of the Association for Natural Language Processing!

The idea is that so-called "prepositions" in Somali are not actually prepositions or adpositions but are either verbal applicative prefix or applicative particle. Although I have never heard "applicative particle" in any other languages, I tentatively assumed it for the sake of the consistency of the Universal Dependencies.
In any case, the distinction between affix and particle (and even clitics!) is highly controversial and I have to look more into it to further discuss the issue.

This research topic first came up in my mind when I was studying Somali under Prof. Martin Orwin in SOAS, University of London. That was one of the best language courses I have had in my life, literally. Although he did mention them as "prepositions", he clearly understood that they behave completely differently from English prepositions. At first I analyzed them in the LFG framework, but then I realized it was not necessarily explained by LFG, but could be analyzed more generally. This idea had not been realized until I decided to write an abstract for an oral presentation at ANLP2021. I did have to write it in a more NLP-ish way, I am very glad that I could make something tangible about my idea. I would say it is difficult for me to pursue this topic, as there is only a scarce amount of data for Somali texts and I even cannot annotate them as I do not have enough knowledge to do that. Still, I would love to resume working on this beautiful language either as my hobby or in terms of linguistics in the near future.
