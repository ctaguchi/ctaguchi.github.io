Who am I?

I am Chihiro Taguchi, a master's student majoring in Natural Language Processing (NLP) at the Nara Institute of Science and Technology (NAIST).
I am a huge language lover, and I study languages as one of my hobbies as well as my research interests.
During my bachelor's study, I conducted a fieldwork to research a linguistic situation on Ikema Island, Okinawa, where a variety of the Miyakoan language, a severely endangered Ryukyuan language, is spoken.
Along with this, I have been studying Tatar and I am particularly interested in Tatar syntax and phonology.
My current research interests include the interface of linguistics and NLP, particularly NLP for minority languages and endangered languages.
Currently I am hoping to work on automatic language detection in Russian–Tatar code-switching texts.
